About the Egyptian Museum

Located in the heart of Cairo's Tahrir Square, the Egyptian Museum stands as the oldest archaeological museum in the Middle East. Established in 1902, it houses the world's most extensive collection of Pharaonic antiquities, offering a window into ancient Egypt's rich history and culture.

Our Mission

The museum is dedicated to exhibiting, documenting, preserving, and promoting artifacts and iconic masterpieces from Egypt's prehistoric era through the Graeco-Roman period.

Historical Background

The concept of preserving Egyptian antiquities dates back to the reign of Muhammad Ali Pasha in the early 19th century. Over the years, the museum's collection has grown, reflecting Egypt's commitment to safeguarding its ancient heritage.

Explore Our Collections

Visitors can explore a vast array of artifacts, including mummies, sarcophagi, jewelry, and everyday items that shed light on the lives of ancient Egyptians. Each exhibit tells a story, connecting the past to the present.
